You weren’t able to help yourself. You threw the door open and then screamed as something hit you, flinging you back into the wall.

Everything went dark.

Weeks later, you woke up in the hospital. “Where am I?” you asked softly.

Rodney was sitting next to you. He flipped through a book and smiled at you when you woke up. “Good morning sleepy head.”

You gave him a repulsed look that made him laugh. It sounded like all the weight in the world was lifting off of his shoulders.

“I’m so glad you’re okay,” he said.

“What happened?”

“Well, when you got knocked out, I sent an emergency signal to our professor. It took a few hours to get a response, which was awful, but she finally got there and then she got you to a hospital.”

“Are my parents here?” you asked meekly.

He shook his head. “They’re at work. I feel responsible for this so I make sure to visit you before school everyday.” He was silent for a moment. “I’m sorry I got you hurt, Y/N. I never should have let you open that door.”

“It’s okay...I don’t know what came over me,” you admitted.

“It was the jewel,” he said.

“Did you get the jewel...?” you asked.

Rodney shook his head. “No, the tunnel collapsed. I was just lucky to get you out in time.”

I sighed. “I’m so sorry.”

“Don’t be,” he said. “There’s no need to be sorry. I’m not done. I’ll fulfill my dream. Just you wait. But now, I have to go to school.” He patted your hand. “I’ll see you there soon, okay?”

You smiled at him. “See ya soon.”

The End.
